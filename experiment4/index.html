Experiment 04 - Infinite Worlds


Description

Our worlds are largely based off of Truchet tiles which can be used to create interesting patterns based off of the negative space in between each colored region. Our first world is utilizing circular paths that interconnect and are entirely monochrome. The second is utilizing right triangles that occupy different sections and angles of their tiles to create their negative space patterns.

Technical

The first world has each tile randomly assigned a type utilizing the hash provided in the example, and that type is then utilized to determine how they interconnect with the surround tile types. A tile either has black circles drawn in its corners, or white circles. When the tiles check their surroundings to determine how to be drawn they create their circular patterns.

The second world has each tile randomly assigned a type also utilizing the hash, and each type also has a unique color. The types decide which corner of the tile they should be drawn in. As a result of not basing any logic on the tiles surroundings, the patterns are less organic and far more random to the eye.

Additionally, both worlds have an animated avatar that is used to traverse the infinite terrain and can follow footprints that are randomly generated on tiles.
